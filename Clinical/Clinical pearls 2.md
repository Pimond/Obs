 * A serum potassium level of 3 mEq/L indicates hypokalemia, which is frequently caused by the use of osmotic diuretics like Mannitol or loop diuretics that promote potassium loss in the urine.
 * The influenza vaccine exhibits a high efficacy rate of 70–90% in healthy individuals under the age of 65, though it typically requires a two-week period following administration for the body to produce a protective antibody response.
 * [[Oseltamivir]] (Tamiflu) is a neuraminidase inhibitor effective for both the chemoprophylaxis and treatment of Influenza Types A and B, ideally administered within 48 hours of symptom onset.
 * High-dose [[Levofloxacin]] (750 mg daily for 5 days) is considered a standard and highly effective short-course treatment for acute pyelonephritis, offering a faster recovery time than traditional 7-day ciprofloxacin regimens.

 * Recurrent Vulvovaginal Candidiasis (VVC) requires a long-term suppressive strategy, typically involving the administration of Fluconazole 150 mg once weekly for a duration of six months.
 * The management of Candida esophagitis necessitates systemic therapy, specifically oral Fluconazole (200–400 mg daily) for 14 to 21 days, as topical agents like Nystatin are ineffective for esophageal involvement.
 * Terbinafine is the gold-standard oral treatment for onychomycosis (nail fungus) due to its superior cure rates and ability to accumulate within the nail plate.
 * Misoprostol is strictly forbidden during pregnancy as it is a prostaglandin analog that induces uterine contractions and can lead to unintended abortion.
 * While long-term Proton Pump Inhibitor (PPI) use increases the risk of pneumonia, fractures, and C. difficile infections, it is associated with hypomagnesemia (low magnesium) rather than hypermagnesemia.
 * Cyproheptadine, an agent with anticholinergic and antihistamine properties, is often used to manage the excessive sweating (diaphoresis) that can occur as a side effect of SSRI therapy.
 * Brexanolone is the first FDA-approved intravenous medication specifically designed to treat postpartum depression, offering a targeted mechanism distinct from standard SSRIs.
 * Alprazolam and other benzodiazepines are not considered first-line treatments for Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD); they are reserved for short-term symptom relief while first-line agents like Fluoxetine or Buspirone take effect.
 * Amitriptyline and other tricyclic antidepressants are generally considered ineffective for the treatment of Social Anxiety Disorder, where SSRIs and SNRIs are the preferred options.
 * Quetiapine is one of the few antipsychotic medications recommended as a monotherapy for the depressive phase of bipolar disorder due to its strong clinical evidence in this specific population.
 * While valproic acid, lithium, and olanzapine are primary first-line choices for acute mania, Carbamazepine is typically reserved as a second-line option.
 * Losartan and other ACE inhibitors or ARBs typically cause "pre-renal" hemodynamic changes rather than Acute Tubular Necrosis (ATN), which is more commonly associated with direct toxins like Tenofovir or Zoledronic acid.

 * Inhaled corticosteroids (ICS) are commonly associated with local side effects like oral thrush and hoarseness, but they do not typically cause systemic metabolic complications like Diabetes Mellitus.
 * Progressive COPD frequently leads to complications such as pulmonary hypertension, cor pulmonale, and polycythemia (elevated red blood cells due to chronic hypoxia), but it does not typically cause anemia.
 * [[Salmeterol]] is a classic Long-Acting Beta-Agonist (LABA) with a significantly longer duration of action compared to short-acting agents like albuterol or terbutaline.
 * [[Captopril]] is used for the long-term management of heart failure but is not indicated for the stabilization of acute decompensated heart failure, where diuretics like furosemide and inotropes like milrinone are required.
 * Interstitial Lung Disease (ILD) is recognized as the most frequent and clinically significant pulmonary manifestation in patients with Rheumatoid Arthritis.
 * Etanercept and certain other TNF-alpha inhibitors are generally considered acceptable for use during pregnancy in patients with Rheumatoid Arthritis, whereas [[Methotrexate]] is strictly contraindicated.
 * Esophagitis and gastrointestinal irritation are the most common side effects reported by patients taking oral [[bisphosphonates]] for osteoporosis.

 * A major and frequent side effect of [[Erythropoietin]] (EPO) therapy in hemodialysis patients is the development of hypertension, not hypotension.

 * [[Empagliflozin]] and other SGLT2 inhibitors increase the risk of vaginal yeast infections by promoting glycosuria, which provides a sugar-rich environment for fungal growth.

 * Ferrous gluconate contains the lowest percentage of elemental iron (12%) compared to ferrous sulfate (20%) or ferrous fumarate (33%).

 * Hypothyroidism, not hyperthyroidism, is a recognized risk factor that increases a patient's susceptibility to statin-induced myopathy.
