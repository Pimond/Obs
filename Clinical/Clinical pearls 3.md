- While short-acting beta-agonists (SABAs) like Salbutamol are the primary rescue agents for asthma, systemic **corticosteroids** (such as [[Prednisolone]]) are critical for managing acute exacerbations to reduce airway inflammation, unlike [[Omalizumab]] or [[Montelukast]], which are strictly for long-term prophylaxis.
- For preventing exercise-induced asthma during high-duration physical activity like a 5-hour mountain climb, a long-acting beta-agonist (LABA) such as **Salmeterol** is recommended because it provides protection for up to 12 hours, whereas Salbutamol only lasts 2–3 hours.
- **Smoking cessation** is the only intervention proven to slow the clinical progression of COPD by halting the accelerated decline in $FEV_1$, while bronchodilators and corticosteroids focus primarily on symptomatic management.
- The most common side effect of the long-acting muscarinic antagonist (LAMA) **[[Tiotropium]]** is **dry mouth** (xerostomia), caused by the blockade of $M_3$ receptors in the salivary glands; this is distinct from the hoarseness and thrush typically associated with inhaled corticosteroids.
- Carbapenems like **[[Imipenem]]** exhibit a significant **post-antibiotic effect (PAE)** against Gram-negative organisms like _Pseudomonas_, allowing them to suppress bacterial growth even after serum concentrations fall below the MIC, a property lacking in most other beta-lactams.
- **[[Amoxicillin]]** is unsuitable for treating prostatitis because it is highly water-soluble and ionized at physiological pH, resulting in poor penetration into the lipid-rich prostatic fluid compared to lipophilic agents like Ciprofloxacin or Doxycycline.
- **[[Penicillin]]** remains the definitive drug of choice for _Streptococcus pyogenes_ (Group A Strep) because this pathogen has never demonstrated clinical resistance to beta-lactams, making alternatives like Azithromycin secondary options.
- **[[Isoniazid]] (INH)** administered for 6–9 months is the classic gold-standard treatment for **latent tuberculosis**, whereas drugs like [[Pyrazinamide]] and Ethambutol are reserved strictly for multi-drug regimens in active TB cases.
- In patients with chronic liver failure, **Pyrazinamide** is generally not recommended because it is the most hepatotoxic of the first-line anti-TB drugs; [[Ethambutol]] is considered the safest alternative as it is primarily cleared by the kidneys.
- Patients using **[[Isotretinoin]]** must avoid blood donation during treatment and for one month after stopping because the drug is highly teratogenic and could cause severe birth defects if the blood is transfused into a pregnant woman.
- Individuals with psoriatic arthritis who test positive on a PPD test must begin latent TB prevention (typically with **Isoniazid**) before or concurrently with starting TNF-alpha inhibitors like **Adalimumab** to prevent the reactivation of the infection.
- **[[Hydroxychloroquine]]** and **[[Etanercept]]** are the preferred DMARD for treating rheumatoid arthritis in patients with Hepatitis C because it lacks the hepatotoxic risks associated with Methotrexate or Leflunomide.
- **Leukopenia** is the most common and dose-limiting hematologic toxicity of **Methotrexate**, occurring because the drug inhibits dihydrofolate reductase and depletes the folate stores necessary for rapidly dividing bone marrow cells.
    
- The concomitant use of statins with **Cyclosporine** significantly increases the risk of myositis and rhabdomyolysis because Cyclosporine inhibits the CYP3A4 and OATP1B1 pathways responsible for statin metabolism and transport.
    
- While corticosteroids, retinoids, and thiazide diuretics can adversely affect lipid profiles, **anticonvulsants** generally do not cause significant hypertriglyceridemia.
    
- **Serology** is useful for the primary diagnosis of _H. pylori_ in untreated patients, but it cannot be used to confirm the eradication of the infection because IgG antibodies can remain positive for years after the bacteria have been cleared.
    
- The duration of acid inhibition from **Proton Pump Inhibitors (PPIs)** is independent of their short serum half-life; instead, it depends on the time required for the body to regenerate new $H^+/K^+$-ATPase pumps after the drug has bound to them irreversibly.
    
- **Regular and NPH insulins** are chemically compatible and can be safely mixed in the same syringe for storage or immediate use, unlike Glargine, which is acidic and will precipitate if mixed with other insulin types.
    
- **Thiazolidinediones (TZDs)** like Pioglitazone are contraindicated in patients with symptomatic heart failure due to fluid retention and in those with osteoporosis due to increased fracture risk, but they are not linked to the development of kidney stones.
    
- **Obesity** ($BMI > 25$) and a **family history** of diabetes are two major, independent risk factors that, when present simultaneously, significantly increase the likelihood of Impaired Glucose Tolerance (IGT).
    
- **Mycophenolate Mofetil** is an immunosuppressant that does not typically cause hypertension, unlike Erythropoietin, NSAIDs, and Cyclosporine, which are frequent secondary causes of elevated blood pressure.
    
- **ACE inhibitors** (or ARBs) combined with a thiazide diuretic are the preferred treatment of choice for secondary stroke prevention in patients with high blood pressure and a history of ischemic stroke.
    
- To prevent rebound tachycardia and myocardial infarction due to receptor up-regulation, beta-blockers like **Carvedilol** should be discontinued gradually, typically by reducing the dose by 25% every week.
    
- For patients with chronic stable angina whose heart rate and blood pressure are already low, **Ranolazine** is the preferred add-on therapy because it reduces ischemic pain by inhibiting the late sodium current without affecting hemodynamic parameters.
    
- In angina patients who maintain a high heart rate despite beta-blocker therapy, adding a non-dihydropyridine calcium channel blocker like **Diltiazem** is preferred to further lower the heart rate and reduce myocardial oxygen demand.
    
- The combination of an SSRI like Sertraline and an MAOI like Tranylcypromine can trigger **Serotonin Syndrome**, a dangerous condition characterized by the triad of autonomic instability, neuromuscular hyperactivity (myoclonus), and altered mental status.
    
- **Mirtazapine** is an ideal antidepressant for patients with low libido, weight loss, and insomnia because its alpha-2 antagonist mechanism promotes sleep and appetite with a very low risk of sexual dysfunction.
    
- For heart failure patients who cannot tolerate ACE inhibitors due to renal failure or hyperkalemia, a combination of **Hydralazine and Isosorbide Dinitrate** is a logical alternative proven to reduce mortality.
    
- Early signs of **Digoxin toxicity** include nausea, vomiting, dizziness, and unique visual disturbances like **yellow-green halos (xanthopsia)**, though photophobia is not a standard symptom of this condition.
    
- The use of **oral contraceptives** can significantly decrease the serum levels of **Lamotrigine** because estrogen induces the UGT enzymes responsible for its glucuronidation, increasing the risk of breakthrough seizures.
    
- **Gabapentin** and Lamotrigine are considered first-line treatments for epilepsy in elderly patients because they are better tolerated and have significantly fewer drug-drug interactions than older agents like Phenytoin.
    
- **Ampicillin** is the preferred antibiotic for urinary tract infections caused by susceptible _Enterococcus faecalis_, with Vancomycin being reserved for resistant strains or patients with penicillin allergies.
    
- In the treatment of acute uncomplicated cystitis, **Nitrofurantoin** requires the longest duration of therapy (5 days) compared to Fosfomycin (single dose) or Cotrimoxazole (3 days).
    
- **Trichomonal vaginitis** is classified as a sexually transmitted infection (STI) that requires treating the partner, whereas bacterial vaginosis and yeast infections are typically caused by an overgrowth of the patient's normal flora.
    
- **Herpes Simplex Virus (HSV)** is the most common cause of painful genital area ulcers worldwide, occurring more frequently than syphilis or chancroid.
    
- **Valproate** therapy in young women is associated with a high risk of weight gain and the development of **Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS)**; furthermore, serum levels above 100-125 mcg/mL increase the likelihood of dose-related toxicity.
    
- **Alprazolam** has no role in the maintenance treatment of Bipolar I disorder because it lacks mood-stabilizing properties and can potentially worsen mood cycling.
    
- **Metronidazole** is the preferred treatment for peritonitis involving _Bacteroides fragilis_ because it provides the most potent anaerobic coverage compared to cephalosporins or clindamycin.
    
- **Buspirone** is an effective anti-anxiety agent that is specifically favored because it lacks addictive potential and does not cause **decreased libido** or sexual dysfunction, unlike many SSRIs.
    
- Among common benzodiazepines, **Oxazepam** has the slowest onset of action because it crosses the blood-brain barrier more slowly than highly lipophilic agents like Diazepam.
    
- Pneumonia resulting from **aspiration** typically involves **anaerobic organisms** from the oropharyngeal flora, distinguishing it from standard community-acquired pneumonia caused by _S. pneumoniae_.
    
- **Clarithromycin** and other macrolides are the treatment of choice for **Mycoplasma pneumonia** because these "atypical" bacteria lack a cell wall, rendering beta-lactams like amoxicillin ineffective.
    
- **Iron deficiency anemia** is not a cause of thrombocytopenia; in fact, it often leads to a reactive increase in platelets (thrombocytosis) as the bone marrow is stimulated.
    
- **Gamma-Glutamyl Transferase (GGT)** is a more specific marker for cholestasis and biliary obstruction than Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP) because GGT is not found in bone tissue and is highly liver-specific.
    
- **Insulin** and beta-adrenergic stimulants like Salbutamol actually cause **hypokalemia** by shifting potassium from the extracellular space into the cells, whereas conditions like malnutrition are a major cause of low magnesium.
    
- Among common iron salts, **Ferrous Fumarate** contains the highest percentage of elemental iron (approximately 33%), compared to Ferrous Sulfate (20%) and Ferrous Gluconate (12%).
    
- **Ferrous sulfate** should not be taken concurrently with drugs like Levodopa, Methyldopa, or Mycophenolate because the iron will chelate these medications in the gut and decrease their absorption; however, it does not interact with **Rivaroxaban**.
    
- For diabetic patients with chronic renal failure and microalbuminuria, the target blood pressure is strictly set at **$< 130/80$** to provide maximum protection against the progression of kidney damage.
    
- While high LDL is a cardiovascular risk, it is not a primary driver of **diabetic nephropathy**; instead, the progression of kidney disease is most affected by poor glycemic control, high blood pressure, and high protein intake.
    
- **Calcium Carbonate** is the preferred first-line phosphate binder for treating hyperphosphatemia in Stage 5 CKD because it is effective, inexpensive, and readily available.
