
- **[[Levofloxacin]]** is highly effective for pneumococcal infections (like pneumonia) because it provides excellent coverage against _Streptococcus pneumoniae_, including penicillin-resistant strains; conversely, **Cefixime** is generally poor against this pathogen, and **Cloxacillin** is primarily reserved for _Staphylococci_.
- **[[Ampicillin-sulbactam]]** is the most appropriate treatment for peritonitis caused by _Enterococcus faecalis_ because ampicillin is the drug of choice for this organism, while sulbactam extends the spectrum to cover anaerobes often present in polymicrobial abdominal infections.
- **Fever** is the hallmark symptom that distinguishes **acute pyelonephritis** (upper UTI) from simple cystitis (lower UTI); while both share symptoms like dysuria and frequency, pyelonephritis is characterized by systemic involvement including high fever, chills, and flank pain.
- **[[Fosfomycin]]** is a convenient single-dose treatment for UTIs, but it generally has **lower or equivalent** clinical efficacy compared to a multi-day course of Nitrofurantoin or SMX-TMP; it is characterized by low GI absorption ($<50\%$) and is excreted unchanged in the urine.
- **Topical [[Benzoyl Peroxide]] (BPO)** should be co-administered with topical antibiotics for acne to significantly reduce the risk of microbial resistance; BPO is a non-antibiotic antimicrobial that prevents _Cutibacterium acnes_ from developing resistance to clindamycin or erythromycin.
- **Coal tar shampoo** is a first-line treatment for limited, itchy psoriatic lesions on the scalp because it acts as a keratolytic and anti-inflammatory agent to remove scales, whereas systemic treatments like Methotrexate are reserved for severe, widespread psoriasis.
- Classic signs of **Trichomoniasis** include a yellow-green, foul-smelling vaginal discharge, a vaginal pH above 4.5 (typically 6.0), and "strawberry spots" (petechiae) on the cervix; the first-line treatment is **Oral [[Metronidazole]]**, with a 7-day course often preferred over a single dose to ensure a higher cure rate.
- For **acute bacterial sinusitis** in patients who cannot tolerate co-amoxiclav due to GI issues, **Respiratory Fluoroquinolones** (like [[Levofloxacin]] or [[Moxifloxacin]]) are the recommended second-line alternatives.
- Hospitalized patients with **Community-Acquired Pneumonia (CAP)** typically receive a combination of a beta-lactam (like **[[Ceftriaxone]]**) to cover typical pathogens and a macrolide (like **[[Azithromycin]]**) to cover atypical organisms like _Legionella_ and _Mycoplasma_.
- A patient with **LDL $\ge$ 190 mg/dL** falls into a high-risk primary prevention category and requires the immediate initiation of **high-intensity statin therapy** (e.g., [[Atorvastatin]] 40–80 mg) regardless of their calculated cardiovascular risk score.
- **Injection site reactions** (redness, swelling, or itching) are the most common adverse effects of [[**PCSK9 inhibitors**]] like [[Alirocumab]], as these are monoclonal antibodies administered via subcutaneous injection.
- When a patient on [[warfarin]] experiences **major bleeding** with a high INR (e.g., 6.5), **oral Vitamin K is NOT recommended** as it acts too slowly; the emergency standard of care requires IV Vitamin K and rapid replacement of clotting factors using Prothrombin Complex Concentrate (PCC) or Fresh Frozen Plasma (FFP).
- In the management of **Heparin-Induced Thrombocytopenia (HIT)**, you must never switch to **[[Enoxaparin]] (LMWH)** because it has nearly 100% cross-reactivity with HIT antibodies; instead, non-heparin anticoagulants like [[Argatroban]] or [[Fondaparinux]] must be used.
- The recommended regimen for **Stage C Heart Failure with reduced Ejection Fraction (HFrEF)** involves the "four pillars": an ARNI (or ACEi/ARB like **[[Valsartan]]**), a Beta-blocker (**[[Bisoprolol]]**), an MRA (**[[Spironolactone]]**), and an SGLT2i.
- **Iron-deficiency anemia** is diagnosed by the triad of low Hemoglobin, low MCV (microcytic), and a critically low **Serum Ferritin** ($<15–30$ ng/mL); in contrast, thalassemias usually present with a normal or high ferritin level.
- Regarding iron therapy, **GI side effects** (nausea, abdominal pain) are dose-dependent based on elemental iron content; notably, **PPIs decrease iron absorption** by increasing gastric pH, and sustained-release forms are generally avoided as they bypass the duodenum (the primary site of absorption).
- **[[Metoprolol]]** (a Beta-blocker) is a key drug for controlling **angina symptoms** because it reduces myocardial oxygen demand by lowering the heart rate and force of contraction.
- **[[Diltiazem]] and [[Verapamil]]** (non-dihydropyridine CCBs) are **NOT appropriate** for patients with heart failure and a reduced ejection fraction ($EF < 40\%$) because their negative inotropic effects can worsen heart failure symptoms.
- **[[Empagliflozin]] (an [[SGLT2 inhibitor]])** leads to polyuria and thirst via osmotic diuresis and significantly increases the risk of **genital mycotic (fungal) infections** because it causes high levels of glucose to be excreted in the urine.
- For a newly diagnosed DM patient with an **HbA1C $> 10\%$**, the best approach is to start **Metformin plus Insulin** to rapidly overcome glucose toxicity and achieve glycemic control.
- **[[Psyllium]] (a bulk-forming laxative)** is the first-line prevention for constipation in pregnancy because it is not systemically absorbed and mimics natural fiber; stimulants like Bisacodyl are only used for short-term "rescue" therapy.
- **Chicory** and **Milk Thistle** are noted for hepatoprotective effects, but **Zinc supplementation** (10–20 mg) is the specific WHO-recommended intervention for children with diarrhea to reduce the severity and duration of the episode.
- **[[Octreotide]]**, a somatostatin analog, is the treatment of choice for diarrhea caused by **carcinoid tumors** because it inhibits the hypersecretion of serotonin and other peptides that cause carcinoid syndrome.
- In **Peptic Ulcer Disease (PUD)**, gastric ulcer pain typically intensifies **immediately after eating** due to acid secretion, whereas duodenal ulcer pain is often **relieved by food**, which acts as a buffer.
- **Standard Triple Therapy for _H. pylori_** requires twice-daily PPI dosing to maintain high gastric pH, which is essential for the stability and efficacy of the co-administered antibiotics ([[Clarithromycin]] and [[Amoxicillin]]).
- **[[Natalizumab]]** is an alpha-4 integrin antagonist that is specifically approved for **Crohn's Disease** but is NOT indicated for Ulcerative Colitis, whereas agents like [[Infliximab]] are used for both.
- **[[Rituximab]]** requires a mandatory **Hepatitis B screen** before initiation because it can cause a fatal reactivation of latent HepB; this is a critical safety "pearl" that differs from the TB screening required for TNF-inhibitors.
- **Glucocorticoids** in Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA) serve as a "bridge therapy" to provide rapid symptom relief but **do not change the disease course** or prevent long-term joint destruction, unlike [[DMARD]]s.
- For mild skin rashes caused by **[[Methimazole]]**, the clinical pearl is to **treat through the symptoms** with an antihistamine rather than switching to PTU, as cross-sensitivity for rashes between these two drugs is very high.
- **[[Teriparatide]]** is a recombinant PTH analog that is **anabolic**, meaning it stimulates new bone formation by osteoblasts; this is the opposite of bisphosphonates, which are anti-resorptive.
- **[[Venlafaxine]] (SNRI)** has one of the highest risks of **withdrawal (discontinuation) syndrome** due to its short half-life; patients often report "brain zaps" and dizziness if they miss a single dose.
- **[[Sertraline]] (SSRI)** is considered the **gold standard** for treating depression in patients with cardiac comorbidities, such as heart failure, due to its superior cardiovascular safety profile.
- **[[Buspirone]]** is effective for Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD) but is generally **ineffective for Panic Disorder**, which requires SSRIs like [[Paroxetine]] or [[Sertraline]].
- **[[Aripiprazole]]** is the best antipsychotic option for obese or dyslipidemic patients because it is **metabolically neutral**, unlike [[Olanzapine]] or [[Clozapine]], which cause significant weight gain.
- **[[Dexamethasone]]** is used for **intractable migraine (status migrainosus)** to reduce the risk of headache recurrence, while **[[Topiramate]]** is a preventative medication that carries a unique risk of **nephrolithiasis** (kidney stones) due to its carbonic anhydrase inhibitory effect.
- **Parenteral [[Aminophylline]]** is no longer recommended for acute asthma attacks due to its narrow therapeutic index and high risk of toxicity compared to modern beta-agonists and corticosteroids.
- **[[Tiotropium]] bromide** is a Long-Acting Muscarinic Antagonist (**LAMA**) that has a much longer duration of action and a lower affinity for M2 receptors (which regulate acetylcholine release) than the short-acting Ipratropium.
- The **FEF25-75%** (Forced Expiratory Flow) is the most sensitive pulmonary function parameter for detecting **small airway obstruction**, often appearing abnormal before the $FEV1/FVC$ ratio drops.
- **[[Fentanyl]]** is the opioid of choice for patients with high histamine sensitivity because it causes significantly **less histamine release** than Morphine or Meperidine, reducing the incidence of drug-induced itching and hypotension.
