* **Peak Plasma Concentration ($C_{max}$)** represents both the rate and extent of a drug's bioavailability, as an increase in the absorption rate typically results in a faster and higher peak, while a greater total extent of absorption also raises the peak concentration.
* According to the **$Q_{10}$ rule** derived from the Arrhenius equation, the degradation rate for most pharmaceutical products increases by a factor of 2 to 3 for every $10^{\circ}\text{C}$ rise in temperature, serving as the fundamental principle for accelerated stability testing.
* **Anti-foaming agents**, such as simethicone, have a characteristic **HLB value of approximately 2**, making them highly lipophilic substances that displace foam-stabilizing surfactants at the air-water interface to collapse bubbles.
* **Lozenges**, also known as troches, are solid dosage forms intended for **oral intra-buccal administration**, where they dissolve slowly in the mouth to provide either a local therapeutic effect or systemic absorption through the buccal mucosa.
* A standard **tablet disintegration apparatus** consists of a basket-rack assembly with six open-ended transparent tubes that move up and down at a constant frequency in a liquid medium maintained at $37^{\circ}\text{C}$.
* **Nitrogen ($N_2$)** is utilized as the drying medium in closed-loop spray dryers to eliminate the risk of explosion when processing flammable organic solvents and to protect heat-sensitive materials from oxidation.
* **Sodium Starch Glycolate** is classified as a superdisintegrant in oral tablet formulations, functioning through the rapid uptake of water and massive swelling to burst the tablet into smaller fragments.
* The addition of **boron compounds (borates)** to glass formulations creates Type I borosilicate glass, which significantly increases the material's hardness, transparency, and chemical durability.
* **Talc** is primarily utilized in tablet manufacturing as a glidant or lubricant to improve powder flow and is not used as a filler because it lacks the necessary compressibility and bulk-forming properties.
* The ideal disintegrant for **vaginal tablets** is a combination of **sodium bicarbonate and an organic acid**, which generates $CO_2$ gas upon contact with minimal moisture to provide the pressure needed to break the tablet apart in low-fluid environments.
* Isotonicity is not essential for **intravenous injections into a large central vein**, such as the vena cava, because the high volume and fast flow of blood rapidly dilute hypertonic solutions, preventing tissue damage.
* The acceptable limit for **friability** in a tablet resistance test is generally **less than 1%**, meaning the tablet must lose no more than 1.0% of its initial weight to be considered durable enough for shipping.
* **Zero-Order drug release** is expected from **osmotic pump systems (OROS)**, which use osmotic pressure to push a drug solution out of a laser-drilled orifice at a constant rate that is independent of the surrounding pH or gastric motility.
* **Type I borosilicate glass** is the highest quality pharmaceutical glass and can be used for all types of preparations, including aqueous parenteral products, due to its superior hydrolytic resistance and chemical inertness.
* A **shell thickness test** is a critical in-process control (IPC) performed during the manufacturing of soft gelatin capsules to ensure the gelatin ribbon is uniform and that the capsules will not leak or break.
* A **high hydroxyl value** in the fatty bases of rectal suppositories indicates a higher concentration of mono- and diglycerides, which improves the **ease of preparation** by enhancing the base's ability to emulsify and incorporate aqueous drugs.
* **Creaming and flocculation** are considered reversible phenomena in an emulsion because the individual droplets remain intact and can be easily redispersed throughout the continuous phase by simple shaking.
* A **subcutaneous (SC) injection** is delivered into the **loose connective and adipose tissues** located immediately beneath the dermis and above the muscle layer, allowing for slow and sustained drug absorption.
* The **attrition (erosion) milling mechanism** is the most efficient for producing particles with good flowability because the scraping action tends to produce rounded or spherical particles that have minimal inter-particulate friction.
* * The **Glass Transition Temperature ($T_g$)** is a characteristic of **amorphous solids**, representing the temperature at which a material shifts from a hard, "glassy" state to a more flexible, "rubbery" state; this differs from **crystalline solids** and their various **polymorphs**, which do not have a $T_g$ but instead exhibit a specific, defined **melting point ($T_m$)**.
* In transdermal drug delivery, the target for systemic absorption is the highly vascularized **dermis**, which contains the capillary beds required for drug entry into the bloodstream, whereas the **stratum corneum** serves as the primary diffusion barrier and the **epidermis** lacks the blood vessels necessary for systemic uptake.

* Reducing a drug's particle size to the **nanoscale** significantly increases its **saturation solubility** and dissolution rate due to a massive increase in the surface-area-to-volume ratio, as explained by the **Ostwald-Freundlich equation**, though it does not alter intrinsic chemical properties like the **$pK_a$**.
* **Ortho-phthalaldehyde (OPA)** is a modern **chemosterilant** frequently used for high-level disinfection of heat-sensitive medical devices like endoscopes; it is often preferred over **Glutaraldehyde** due to its faster action and lower irritation, and is distinct from **Formaldehyde**, which is primarily used as a gaseous sterilant.
* **Deliquescent materials** are substances with such high hygroscopicity that they absorb sufficient atmospheric moisture to eventually dissolve completely and form a liquid solution, a property that is distinct from simple low-melting-point solids or standard crystalline materials.
* The entrapment of water or solvent molecules within a drug's crystalline lattice results in the formation of **hydrates** or **solvates** (often termed **pseudopolymorphism**), whereas **polymorphism** refers to a drug existing in different crystalline forms without the inclusion of any solvent molecules.
* **Medicinal tinctures** are alcoholic or hydroalcoholic solutions prepared by the extraction of plant or animal drugs, distinguishing them from **spirits**, which are solutions of volatile oils in alcohol, or **syrups**, which are aqueous sugar-based preparations.
* **Lyophilized (freeze-dried) tablets** possess a highly porous "honeycomb" structure that allows for **rapid dissolution** the moment they contact moisture, unlike **enteric-coated tablets** designed for intestinal release or **sustained-release** systems that delay dissolution.
* **Zero-order drug release**, where the drug is released at a constant rate over time, is effectively achieved using **osmotic pumps** or reservoir diffusion systems, whereas standard tablets containing **water-soluble drugs** typically follow **first-order kinetics**, where the rate decreases as the drug concentration drops.
* Based on the **Noyes-Whitney equation**, 
	$\frac{dC}{dt} = \frac{DA}{h} (C_s - C)$
	the dissolution rate is directly proportional to the **saturation solubility ($C_s$)** and the **surface area ($A$)**, while it remains inversely proportional to the thickness of the stagnant diffusion layer ($h$).
* **Kinematic viscosity ($\nu$)** is defined as the **ratio of dynamic (absolute) viscosity ($\eta$) to density ($\rho$)**, expressed as $\nu = \frac{\eta}{\rho}$; this is distinct from **relative viscosity**, which compares a solution's viscosity to its solvent, or **specific viscosity**, which measures the relative increase caused by a solute.
* **DMSO (Dimethyl Sulfoxide)** acts as a potent skin penetration enhancer by disrupting the lipid-protein structure of the stratum corneum, thereby increasing the **diffusion coefficient** of the drug within the skin.
* The primary role of **cholesterol** in liposomal formulations is to act as a **membrane stabilizer**, filling gaps between phospholipids to reduce membrane permeability and increase **drug retention**, preventing the liposome from "leaking" its contents.
* **Eudragit S** is a pH-sensitive enteric polymer used for selective drug delivery to the **colon** because it only dissolves at $pH > 7.0$, whereas general binders like **HPMC** or **CMC** do not offer such specific site-targeting capabilities.
* In pharmaceutical suspensions, the **DLVO theory** explains the stability of the system by calculating the balance between attractive **van der Waals forces** and repulsive **electrostatic forces** between particles, while **Stokes' Law** is used specifically to predict the sedimentation rate.
* According to **Darcy's Law**, the **filtration rate** is directly proportional to the **pressure difference ($\Delta P$)** across the filter medium, whereas it is inversely proportional to the viscosity of the liquid and the thickness of the filter cake.
* Adding an alcohol as a **co-solvent** to water lowers the dielectric constant of the medium, which reduces its ability to separate ions and subsequently leads to a **decrease in the degree of ionic dissociation** of the drug.
* **Paddle mixers** are ideally suited for mixing **high-viscosity liquids** because they operate at low speeds with a large surface area; in contrast, high-speed **propellers** or air jets are only suitable for low-viscosity fluids.
* A **colloidal mill** utilizes high-shear rotor-stator forces to reduce particle size, making it the preferred device for preparing stable **semi-solids and suspensions**, whereas ball mills or hammer mills are typically used for grinding dry solids.
* **Butyl Hydroxy Anisole (BHA)** is a phenolic **antioxidant** used to prevent the oxidative degradation of fats and oils in various formulations, a role distinct from that of **co-solvents** like glycerin or **sweeteners** like aspartame.
* Vegetable oils such as **soybean, cottonseed, and sunflower oil** are suitable for injectable emulsions because they are biodegradable; conversely, **paraffin oil (mineral oil)** is a hydrocarbon that is not metabolizable and can cause **granulomas** if injected.
* **Castor oil** is added to **collodions** to act as a **plasticizer**, ensuring that the resulting pyroxylin film remains flexible and does not crack after the volatile solvents (ether and alcohol) have evaporated.
* Bacteria tolerate an **isotonic environment** ($0.9\% \text{ NaCl}$) best, as there is no net movement of water across the cell membrane; exposure to **hypertonic** solutions ($7.5\% \text{ NaCl}$) causes cell shriveling (**plasmolysis**), while **hypotonic** solutions ($0.09\% \text{ NaCl}$) cause cell bursting (**plasmoptysis**).
* The **D-value (Decimal Reduction Time)** represents the time required at a specific temperature to kill **$90\%$** of a microbial population (one log-cycle); the **Z-value** refers to the temperature change required to alter the D-value by a factor of 10.
* The most significant disadvantage of **fixed-bed (static) dryers** is the **migration phenomenon**, where moisture moving to the surface carries dissolved drugs or dyes with it, leading to uneven drug distribution and potential **mottling** in the final product.
