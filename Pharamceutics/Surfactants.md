| **Type**       | **Class**               | **Examples**                                           | **Main Applications**                                        | **Unique Properties**                                                                                               |
| -------------- | ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Anionic**    | Negatively charged head | **Sodium Lauryl Sulfate (SLS)**, Sodium Stearate       | Wetting agents, detergents, shampoo, O/W emulsifiers.        | High foaming capacity; can be irritating to skin/mucosa; incompatible with cationic drugs.                          |
| **Cationic**   | Positively charged head | **Benzalkonium Chloride (BKC)**, Cetrimide             | **Preservatives**, disinfectants, antiseptic skin cleansers. | Strong antimicrobial properties; adsorbed onto glass/plastic; incompatible with anionic surfactants (SLS).          |
| **Non-ionic**  | Uncharged (most common) | **Spans (Sorbitan esters)**, **Tweens (Polysorbates)** | Emulsifiers, solubilizers, stabilizers for proteins.         | **Low toxicity**; compatible with most drugs; Tweens are water-soluble (high HLB), Spans are oil-soluble (low HLB). |
| **Non-ionic**  | Poloxamers              | **Pluronic F-127**                                     | Gelling agents, solubilizers, nanocarriers.                  | **Thermoreversible gelling**: liquid at cold temps, solidifies at body temp.                                        |
| **Amphoteric** | Both +/- charges        | **Lecithin**, Cocamidopropyl betaine                   | Natural emulsifier, parenteral (IV) emulsions, liposomes.    | Derived from egg yolk or soy; very biocompatible; pH-dependent charge (zwitterionic).                               |
