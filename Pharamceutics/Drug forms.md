## Hydroalcoholic

| **Dosage Form** | **Key Characteristics**                                                                                       | **Typical Use**                                                                                        |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Collodion**   | A solution of **pyroxylin** (nitrocellulose) in a mixture of **ethanol and ethyl ether**.                     | When applied to the skin, the solvent evaporates, leaving a protective film (like a "liquid bandage"). |
| **Tincture**    | Alcoholic or hydroalcoholic solutions prepared from **vegetable materials** or chemical substances.           | Usually for internal or external use (e.g., Tincture of Iodine).                                       |
| **Spirit**      | Alcoholic or hydroalcoholic solutions of **volatile** substances (usually volatile oils).                     | often used as flavoring agents or for their medicinal value (e.g., Peppermint Spirit).                 |
| **Liniment**    | Liquid or semi-liquid preparations intended for external application to the skin **with friction** (rubbing). | Used for muscle pain relief (rubefacient). Can be alcoholic or oily.                                   |
